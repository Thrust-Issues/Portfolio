01_about

How I approach engineering

Systems don't fail where we expect them to — they fail where we stop questioning our assumptions. That's the lesson that has shaped how I work: models and simulations don't provide answers, they reflect the quality of the questions behind them. It's why I care as much about validating a design through testing and analysis as I do about the initial CAD.

I grew up in Puerto Rico, where a lot of students don't encounter real STEM opportunities until much later than they should. That's part of why I'm pursuing mechanical engineering — and eventually a PhD — with the goal of doing research rigorous enough to matter and open enough for students like the one I was to find their way into it.

Currently a Mechanical Engineering Intern at the Bio-Inspired Perception and Robotics Laboratory (BPRL), Co-Leader of the Payload Design and Manufacture Division at LIFTS, and working on the Hardware Division of ROCS Carpool.
